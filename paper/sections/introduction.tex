\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:introduction}

Monetary policy announcements by the Federal Reserve are among the most closely watched events in financial markets. The Federal Open Market Committee (FOMC) meets eight times per year to set the target federal funds rate and communicate its economic outlook. These announcements have the potential to resolve uncertainty about the future path of monetary policy, which may be reflected in changes to implied volatility as measured by the CBOE Volatility Index (VIX).

During our sample period (2011--2018), only a subset of FOMC meetings were followed by a press conference given by the Fed Chair. This institutional feature creates a natural split that allows us to test whether the presence of a press conference---and the additional information it conveys---affects the market's volatility response.

This paper documents the behavior of the VIX around FOMC announcement dates and tests whether meetings with press conferences are associated with a different VIX response than meetings without them. A more detailed discussion of the motivation and related literature will be provided in a future revision.
