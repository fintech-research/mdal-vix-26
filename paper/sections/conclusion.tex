\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

This paper documents a systematic difference in the VIX response to FOMC announcements depending on whether the meeting is accompanied by a press conference. Over the 2011--2018 sample, meetings with press conferences are associated with larger VIX declines, while meetings without press conferences tend to see VIX increases in the days following the announcement. These preliminary findings are consistent with the hypothesis that press conferences help resolve market uncertainty beyond what is conveyed by the policy statement alone.

Future work will extend this analysis by formally testing the statistical significance of the press-conference effect, exploring additional control variables, and discussing the results in the context of the related literature.
